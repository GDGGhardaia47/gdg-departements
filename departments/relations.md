## 1. The Foundation (For All Department Members)
Before specializing, every member of the Relations Department masters the art of professional management:
- **Professional Communication**: You will unlearn "texting" habits and learn how to write high-stakes business emails, structure proposals, and present the GDG value proposition clearly.
- **Google Workspace Mastery**: We run on Google products. You will master Docs, Sheets, and Slides not just for homework, but for real-time collaboration and project management.
- **GDG Branding**: Understanding who we are, what we stand for, and how to represent the global Google Developer Group identity correctly.

## 2. The Specializations (Choose Your Path)
After the basics, you will focus on one of these three tracks:

### Track A: External Relations
* **Focus**: Building bridges with companies, speakers, and sponsors to support the club.
* **What you will do**: Scouting for partners, writing sponsorship proposals, and mastering the art of negotiation.
* **Key Projects**:
    - **Contact Database**: Building and managing a professional network using Google Contacts (Sponsors, Partners, Speakers, ...).
    - **Simulation**: A "Sponsorship Gathering" workshop where you practice pitching to a CEO.
    - **Negotiation**: Learning how to secure funding and venue deals.

### Track B: Marketing
* **Focus**: Getting the word out. While Design creates the visuals, you create the *strategy* to make sure people see them.
* **What you will do**: Managing social media algorithms, planning content calendars, and email marketing.
* **Key Projects**:
    - **Campaign Simulation**: Running a full marketing plan for a mock event.
    - **Brand Guardianship**: Ensuring every post follows the official Google Brand Guidelines.
    - **Analytics**: Learning to use Meta Business Suite to see what works and what doesn't.

### Track C: Events & Logistics
* **Focus**: Turning plans into reality. You are the ones running the show on the ground.
* **What you will do**: Detailed event planning, creating step-by-step agendas, and managing the physical venue.
* **Key Projects**:
    - **The Master Plan**: Creating comprehensive checklists and timelines using Google Sheets.
    - **Crisis Management**: A simulation workshop assigning roles (Welcoming, Sound, Logistics) to handle real-time event pressure.
    - **Template Creation**: Building standard forms for smooth operations.

## 3. What You Will Learn
- **Negotiation**: How to ask for support or collaboration and get a "Yes."
- **Project Management**: How to organize a complex event with hundreds of attendees without forgetting a single detail.
- **Digital Strategy**: Understanding how social media algorithms work and how to grow an audience.
- **Crisis Resolution**: How to stay calm and fix problems when the microphone breaks or the food is late.

## 4. Contribution to the Club
- **Sustainability**: You bring in the resources that keep the club alive.
- **Growth**: You are responsible for filling the room; without you, speakers talk to empty chairs.
- **Experience**: You ensure the attendees are happy, comfortable, and welcomed from the moment they walk in.
- **Reputation**: The professionalism of your emails and organization defines how the university and companies see us.

## 5. Career Impact (Future Jobs)
- **Project Manager**: Organizing resources and timelines is the core of this high-paying role.
- **Public Relations (PR) Specialist**: Managing the image and connections of a company.
- **Digital Marketer**: Running campaigns and social media strategies.
- **Event Planner**: Coordinating conferences and professional gatherings.
